Historical origin

The United Nations emblem was created to serve as the institutional insignia of the UN, visually representing the organisation's global mandate for peace, security and international cooperation. It became widely associated with UN peacekeeping, humanitarian assistance and representative missions.

Legal context and misuse

The United Nations controls the use of its emblem and name through regulations that protect the organisation’s neutrality and authority. Unauthorised use may constitute false endorsement or misrepresentation and is restricted or penalised in many jurisdictions.
