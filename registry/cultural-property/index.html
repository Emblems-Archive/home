Historical origin

The cultural property protection emblem (a white shield with blue markings) was established by the Hague Convention for the Protection of Cultural Property in the Event of Armed Conflict (1954) to identify and safeguard monuments, collections and sites of cultural significance during hostilities.

Legal context and misuse

The Hague Convention and its Protocols define the protective regime for cultural property. Unauthorised marking, commercial exploitation, or deceptive use of the emblem undermines protection and is often prohibited by national implementing legislation.
