Historical origin

The Red Cross is the oldest and most widely recognised protective sign in modern humanitarian law. Introduced in the diplomatic process that produced the First Geneva Convention (1864), it marks and protects medical units, hospitals and authorised humanitarian personnel during armed conflict. The emblem intentionally inverts the colours of the Swiss national flag as a deliberate reference to the movement’s Swiss origins and tradition of neutrality.

Legal context and misuse

The Red Cross emblem is strictly protected under the Geneva Conventions and national implementation statutes. Unauthorised commercial or decorative use dilutes the emblem’s protective value and may be sanctioned. In digital culture, including multiplayer games such as Among Us, developers typically avoid reproducing exact Geneva emblems and instead use altered or substitute icons to prevent unauthorised use and legal exposure.
