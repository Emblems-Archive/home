Civil Defense

[ PROTECTED ]

Historical Origin

The civil defense sign, a blue triangle on an orange ground, was codified in modern humanitarian treaty law to identify civil defense organizations, personnel, and designated facilities during conflict and emergency response operations.

Legal context and misuse

The civil defence sign is codified in treaty practice (notably Additional Protocol I) as an identifier for civil protection services and receives legal safeguarding for protective identification. Unauthorised or decorative use may endanger operational clarity during emergencies and can be restricted by domestic law. In digital and entertainment media, exact protected symbols are frequently altered to avoid misrepresentation.
