Special Sign for Dangerous Forces

Historical origin

The special sign for dangerous forces (commonly shown as three orange circles) was developed to identify facilities whose damage would produce severe dangers to civilian populations—such as dams, reservoirs, and certain industrial or nuclear installations. It supports operational decisions on precaution and protection in armed conflict.

Legal context and misuse

The sign is referenced in treaty practice relating to installations containing dangerous forces (notably Additional Protocol I) and guides protective measures. Unauthorised or misleading use can compromise civilian safety and may breach domestic and international obligations on distinction and proportionality.
