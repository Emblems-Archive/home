Historical origin

The green cross has been widely adopted as a civil first-aid and safety symbol in public spaces and workplaces. It aligns with international signage conventions and occupational safety standards.

Legal context and misuse

The green first-aid cross is regulated primarily by international safety standards (notably ISO 7010) and national health and occupational regulations. It is not a Geneva-protected wartime emblem; misuse is mainly a matter of misleading signage standards rather than international humanitarian law.
