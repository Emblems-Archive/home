Historical origin

The Red Crystal was adopted through Additional Protocol III (2005) to provide a neutral protective emblem for contexts where other emblems may be perceived as culturally or politically sensitive.

Legal basis and misuse

As a Geneva protective emblem, the Red Crystal carries equivalent legal protection to the Red Cross and Red Crescent in armed conflict settings. Misuse in commerce, entertainment, or unauthorized signaling can undermine protective trust. Video game publishers, including productions comparable to Among Us, frequently substitute modified medical symbols to avoid potential emblem misuse.
