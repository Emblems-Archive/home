Historical origin

The Rod of Asclepius traces to classical antiquity as a symbol associated with healing and medicine. It has been adopted in modern times as an emblem for medical organisations, professional bodies and healthcare services.

Legal context and misuse

The Rod of Asclepius is a traditional medical symbol rooted in classical antiquity and is not protected by the Geneva Conventions as a wartime emblem. Its display is governed primarily by professional, institutional and trademark rules rather than international humanitarian law.
